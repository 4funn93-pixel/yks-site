## The simplest habit tracker that actually works

Most habit apps fail for the same reason: they add friction to the exact moment you're trying to make effortless. This is the opposite. One page, one pen, five minutes.

You write your habits down the left column, and each day you put an **X** in the box. That's the whole system. The visible row of X's becomes a chain — and after a week or two, *not breaking the chain* turns into its own quiet motivation.

### How to use it

1. **Print one page per month.** Write the month at the top.
2. **List 2–3 habits** to start (you can track up to 11, but don't). Be specific: "10 min walk" beats "exercise".
3. **Mark each box** the day you do the habit. Miss a day? No guilt — just don't miss two in a row.
4. **At month end,** glance at your rows. The patterns tell you which habits are sticking and which need to be smaller.

### Why paper beats an app here

- **No notifications, no streak anxiety, no dopamine slot machine.** Just a quiet record.
- **It lives where you can see it** — on the fridge, the desk, the wall. Visibility is most of the battle.
- **Filling a box by hand is a tiny reward** your brain actually registers.

> The goal isn't a perfect grid. It's a slightly better month than the last one. Print it, stick it somewhere you'll see it, and start with tomorrow.
