## A calendar that gets out of the way

Wall calendars are either cluttered with stock photos and tiny boxes, or they cost more than they should. This one is the opposite: twelve clean pages, one month each, with room to actually write.

It's designed to **disappear into your wall or desk** and just do its job — show you the month and give you space to plan it.

### What you get

- **12 pages**, January–December 2026, one month per page
- **Monday-start grid** with large, open day cells
- **Ink-light design** — thin gridlines, black text, no heavy fills
- Prints cleanly at **US Letter or A4**, portrait

### A few ways people use it

- **On the wall** by the door, for the whole household to see the month.
- **At the desk**, one month at a time, swapped out as you go.
- **As a project map** — print the relevant months and block out milestones.

> Print the month you're in, pin it where you'll pass it, and fill it in by hand. A calendar you can see and touch tends to get used; one buried in an app gets ignored.
